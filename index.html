What do they eat in Spain. Most popular food and drinks

The Spaniards, like all southern peoples, are very fond of food and are never shy about it. And it is very difficult to blame them for their love of food: their national cuisine is filled with delicious dishes made from fish, meat and rice, and it is simply impossible to refuse “one more piece”. And a table laid by generous Spaniards and decorated with excellent wine or sherry is an unforgettable taste experience. 1-Paella So, the most popular Spanish dish, which today you can try in many restaurants in any country in the world, is the famous Spanish paella (paella). This is rice that is fried with saffron and other spices, and then poultry and / or seafood are added to it. And, in fact, the combination of chicken and seafood at first glance may not seem too tasty. This is not true. The Spaniards claim that there are more than three hundred different types of paella, but in most restaurants, of course, you will not find such a variety - the maximum that can be found on the menu is 5 or 6 types of dishes. So, in the usual menu you can find paella with or without chicken meat, with cuttlefish ink, which, as you know, give the dish a black color, or paella with a crust - it is prepared not with seafood, but with sausages.

Fabada

From hearty dishes in Spain, you need to eat this first thing. Fragrant, in a good way fatty, very satisfying - it rarely leaves anyone indifferent. Fabada is made from white beans with bacon, pork ham, chorizo ​​with paprika and saffron. It is customary to serve beer, cider or wine. Spaniards most often eat this dish in winter - for the summer heat such food is heavy. Tourists, on the other hand, try fabada in Spain all year round; in restaurants of national cuisine, it is present in any season. To repeat this dish at home, bring Asturian white beans, paprika, saffron, spicy smoked sausages from Spain. All this is easy to find in supermarkets, but it is more profitable to buy spices in the markets.

Pisto

The Spanish version of ratatouille, in hotels this dish is often served for breakfast. Pisto consists of tomatoes, peppers, onions, zucchini or eggplant - all stewed in olive oil with spices. It is served warm, most often with scrambled eggs and toast. Pisto in Spain can be ordered as an independent dish (a great option for vegetarians) or as a side dish for fish. It is believed that its best version is prepared in the Basque Country: in Bilbao you can try Pisto a la Bilbaína, a vegetable stew in tomato sauce, served with scrambled eggs, often complemented by sausages and bacon.
